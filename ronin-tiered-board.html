Three Tiers, Six Rings

Outer / middle / inner tiers of two rings each, Emperor at the heart. Stones grow with radius so the gaps stay even from centre to edge.

Tap or hover a stone — gold shows its neighbours
OUTER TIER · R1–2 MIDDLE · R3–4 INNER · R5–6 EMPEROR

The sizing trick: each ring's stone radius is a fixed fraction of that ring's own arc spacing — the actual distance between neighbouring stones on that ring. Because outer rings round their counts to multiples of 4, their spacing varies slightly, and the stones now absorb that variance instead of leaving uneven gaps.

Tier logic maps straight onto your square game: outer tier = outer wall, middle tier = courtyard, inner tier = keep. Guards could be bound to a tier, gates could sit at tier boundaries, and the ronin's journey reads as three escalating zones — exactly the structure your current board implies, bent into a circle.
